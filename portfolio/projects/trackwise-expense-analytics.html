TrackWise — Expense Analytics Platform

An AI-powered expense and financial analytics platform that simplifies business expense management by automating bill and invoice processing, transaction tracking, financial analysis, and performance monitoring — starting from a scanned invoice, not a manually typed spreadsheet row.

Context
Internship build, Jun–Oct 2025
Input
Scanned invoices & bills
Output
Financial dashboards
Status
Internal, not publicly deployed

01 Overview

TrackWise turns a physical or scanned invoice into structured financial data automatically. Instead of someone manually re-typing vendor names, amounts, and dates into a spreadsheet, the platform reads the document itself, extracts the relevant fields, and files the transaction into the right place — ready for reporting the moment it's scanned.

02 The problem

Manual invoice entry is slow and error-prone, and it's usually the first place financial reporting breaks down — a mistyped amount or a miscategorized vendor throws off every dashboard built on top of it. The goal was to remove that manual step entirely: scan the document, extract the data correctly the first time, and let the analytics layer stay accurate by construction.

03 Architecture

01
OCR ingestion pipeline
Scans invoices and bills, extracting vendor, amount, date, and line-item data directly from the document image.
02
Transaction categorization
Automatically classifies extracted transactions so they roll up correctly into income, expense, and vendor reports without manual tagging.
03
MySQL data store
Every extracted and categorized record is stored relationally, making it queryable for both dashboards and ad-hoc analysis.
04
Analytics dashboards
Surfaces income, expenses, profit/loss, vendor analytics, and spending trends from the categorized transaction data.

The OCR and backend logic is built in Java and C++, data is persisted in MySQL, and results are exposed through a REST API consumed by an HTML reporting front end.

04 What it handles

  • OCR-based invoice and bill scanning
  • Automatic transaction categorization
  • Income, expense, and profit/loss reporting
  • Vendor analytics and spending-trend dashboards
  • Financial performance monitoring over time
